About Me

My introduction to programming and application development started during my employment with Engman-Taylor in Appleton, Wisconsin. During my employment I used access to automate the generation of hazardous materials issued, and obtained my first experience with databases and applications. I went on to repeat this process while employed with Bear Island Paper Company in Doswell, Virginia. There I created a fully automated system to print labels and create filing materials for recycled paper purchasing.


I am currently employed as a programmer and service manager for Advanced Scale Company where I program scales using the iRite language. I provide fully automated scale systems and software to automate production processes and setup of manufacturing equipment. My decision to learn full stack web development was a natural transition, as my systems became more complex and customer needs for data tracking exceeded my ability to deliver.


I am currently a student at Georgia Tech Savannah and I am looking forward to completing my full stack web development courses. After completion of these courses I will be able to deliver reliable and scalable systems that interface with hardware to provide complete business solutions for manufacturing and other industries. My diverse skill set will enable me to provide solutions that don't currently exist, and I look forward to keeping my skillset up to date with the latest technologies.
